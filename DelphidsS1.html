Dolphins are the smallest toothed whales and they are also the most diverse group of cetaceans. They occupy rivers, coasts, and oceans, but are generally restricted to the tropics. Originating more than 30 million years ago, they have evolved distinct characteristics like a large cavity on their heads to produce complex sounds. Experiments in captivity have shown dolphins to be extremely intelligent, and this argument has in-turn made us question their captivity. Additionally, while coastal dolphins sometimes show seasonal movements, oceanic animals may move hundreds of kilometres in a day in search of food. Therefore, many organisations and governments are pushing towards banning dolphinariums.

Dolphins form complex bonds with other individuals and even other species and may have intricate societal structures just like we do. Their curious and playful nature has elicited great empathy from people all over the world and turned many species into ambassadors of their ecosystems, aiding in the conservation of natural spaces.
